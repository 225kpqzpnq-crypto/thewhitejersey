# Rowing Training: Pre & Post Mobility and Recovery Checklist

## PRE-ROWING MOBILITY PROTOCOL (10-15 minutes total)

### Phase 1: General Warm-Up (3-5 minutes)
Raise core temperature before any stretching or mobilisation.

- [ ] Light aerobic activity (jogging, skipping, or easy rowing at very low intensity)
- [ ] Target: slight increase in heart rate and body temperature
- [ ] Should feel warmer but not fatigued

### Phase 2: Dynamic Mobility Drills (5-8 minutes)
Move through each position with control—no bouncing or forcing range.

**Lower Body**

- [ ] **3-Way Ankle Mobility** — 10 reps each direction per ankle
  - Circles, dorsiflexion rocks, lateral tilts
- [ ] **Bodyweight Squats** — 10-15 reps, focus on depth and control
- [ ] **Hip Hinges** — 10-12 reps, practice the movement pattern used at body-over
- [ ] **Walking Lunges with Rotation** — 6-8 per side
  - Step forward, rotate torso toward front leg
- [ ] **Leg Swings** — 10 forward/back, 10 side-to-side per leg
- [ ] **3-Way Hip Opener** — 10 reps per position
  - Hip circles, 90/90 transitions, pigeon rocks

**Upper Body & Spine**

- [ ] **Arm Circles** — 10 forward, 10 backward (controlled, not flailing)
- [ ] **Shoulder Rolls** — 10 forward, 10 backward
- [ ] **Thoracic Rotations** — 8-10 per side
  - On all fours: thread needle movement (arm slides under body, then reaches to ceiling)
- [ ] **Cat-Cow** — 10 cycles for spinal mobility
- [ ] **Band Pull-Aparts** (if available) — 15 reps
- [ ] **Pushup-Plus** — 10 reps (protract shoulder blades at top of pushup position)

**Rowing-Specific**

- [ ] **Hip Flexor Stretch (dynamic)** — 30 seconds per side
  - Deep lunge, gentle pulses—critical given compression demands at the catch
- [ ] **World's Greatest Stretch** — 5-6 per side
  - Lunge position, elbow to instep, rotate and reach to ceiling
- [ ] **Hands-Overhead Hinge** — 10 reps
  - Arms extended overhead, hinge at hips maintaining neutral spine

### Phase 3: On-Erg/On-Water Progression (5-10 minutes)
Gradual build to working intensity—do not skip this.

- [ ] **Arms Only** — 1-2 minutes at very low pressure
- [ ] **Arms + Body Swing** — 1-2 minutes
- [ ] **Half Slide** — 1-2 minutes
- [ ] **Three-Quarter Slide** — 1-2 minutes
- [ ] **Full Slide at Low Intensity** — 2-4 minutes
- [ ] **Build pieces** — 3-4 x 10 strokes with progressive intensity
- [ ] Optional: Include 1-2 "Power 10s" if preparing for high-intensity work

## POST-ROWING RECOVERY PROTOCOL

### Immediate: Cool-Down (5-10 minutes)

**On-Erg/On-Water**
- [ ] **Easy rowing** — 3-5 minutes at decreasing intensity
- [ ] Focus on technique refinement while heart rate drops
- [ ] Stroke rate 16-20, minimal pressure

**Transition**
- [ ] **Light walking** — 2-3 minutes (especially important for home ergers who might otherwise go straight to sedentary)

### Static Stretching (5-10 minutes)
Hold each stretch 20-30 seconds, no bouncing. Muscles are warm—this is the optimal time for flexibility development.

**Priority Areas for Rowers**

- [ ] **Hamstrings** — lying or seated stretch, 30 seconds per leg
  - Critical for catch position and hip hinge mechanics
- [ ] **Hip Flexors** — kneeling lunge stretch, 30 seconds per side
  - Counteracts compression at the catch
- [ ] **Glutes/Piriformis** — figure-4 stretch or pigeon pose, 30 seconds per side
- [ ] **Quadriceps** — standing or side-lying quad stretch, 30 seconds per leg
- [ ] **Calves** — wall stretch or step drop, 30 seconds per leg
- [ ] **Lower Back** — child's pose or knees-to-chest, 30-60 seconds
- [ ] **Thoracic Spine** — open book stretch (lying on side, rotating arm overhead), 10 reps per side
- [ ] **Lats/Shoulders** — doorway stretch or overhead reach with side bend, 30 seconds per side
- [ ] **Chest/Pectorals** — doorway stretch (arms at varying heights), 30 seconds
- [ ] **Neck** — gentle lateral neck stretches (ear to shoulder), 20 seconds per side
  - Never use a foam roller directly on the neck

### Foam Rolling (Optional but Recommended, 5-10 minutes)
Use after cool-down, before static stretching. Roll slowly—approximately 1 minute per muscle group. Pause on tender spots for 30 seconds.

- [ ] **Quadriceps** — roll from hip to just above knee
- [ ] **Hamstrings** — roll from glutes to just above knee (cross legs for more pressure)
- [ ] **IT Band** — roll from hip to knee on lateral thigh
- [ ] **Calves** — roll from ankle to below knee
- [ ] **Glutes** — sit on roller, cross one ankle over opposite knee
- [ ] **Upper Back/Thoracic** — roll from mid-back to shoulders (arms crossed or extended)
- [ ] **Lats** — lie on side, roller under armpit, roll toward hip

**Do NOT foam roll:**
- Directly on the spine
- The neck
- Over bony prominences
- Areas with acute injury or inflammation

## HYDRATION PROTOCOL

### Pre-Training
- [ ] Arrive well-hydrated (urine should be pale yellow)
- [ ] Drink 400-600ml water in the 2-3 hours before training

### During Training (sessions >60 minutes)
- [ ] Sip 150-200ml every 15-20 minutes
- [ ] Target: 400-800ml per hour
- [ ] For sessions >60-90 minutes or high sweat conditions: consider electrolyte drink

### Post-Training (Critical Window)
- [ ] **Weigh yourself before and after training** — weight loss reflects fluid loss
- [ ] **Replace at 1.5x the weight lost** (e.g., 1kg lost = 1.5L fluid needed)
- [ ] Include electrolytes (sodium, potassium) to aid absorption
- [ ] Options: water with electrolyte tablet, coconut water, sports drink, milk
- [ ] Spread intake over 2-4 hours rather than consuming all at once

**Signs of Dehydration to Monitor:**
- Dark urine
- Headache
- Fatigue disproportionate to session intensity
- Decreased performance
- Muscle cramping

## NUTRITION/FUELLING PROTOCOL

### Pre-Training (Fuelling)

**3-4 hours before:**
- [ ] Balanced meal with complex carbohydrates, lean protein, moderate fat
- [ ] Examples: oatmeal with fruit and eggs, wholegrain toast with peanut butter and banana, rice with chicken and vegetables
- [ ] Target: primarily carbohydrate-based to top up glycogen stores

**30-60 minutes before (if needed):**
- [ ] Small, easily digestible snack (100-200 calories)
- [ ] Examples: banana, toast with honey, small handful of dried fruit
- [ ] Avoid high fat/fibre/protein close to training (slows digestion)

### Post-Training (Recovery Window)

**Within 30-60 minutes (Critical):**
The glycogen replenishment window is most effective immediately post-exercise. Waiting >1 hour reduces glycogen restoration by approximately 50%.

- [ ] **Carbohydrate target:** 0.8-1.2g per kg body weight
  - 70kg athlete = 56-84g carbohydrate
- [ ] **Protein target:** 20-30g to support muscle repair
- [ ] **Ratio:** approximately 3:1 carbohydrate to protein

**Quick Post-Training Options:**
- [ ] Chocolate milk (convenient carb:protein ratio)
- [ ] Greek yoghurt with fruit and granola
- [ ] Banana with peanut butter
- [ ] Recovery shake (whey protein + fruit + oats)
- [ ] Sandwich with lean protein

**Within 2 hours:**
- [ ] Full balanced meal
- [ ] Complex carbohydrates (rice, pasta, potatoes, bread)
- [ ] Lean protein (chicken, fish, eggs, tofu, legumes)
- [ ] Vegetables for micronutrients
- [ ] Include some healthy fats

### Daily Considerations for Rowers
- [ ] Eat protein consistently throughout the day (every 3-4 hours) to prevent muscle breakdown
- [ ] Higher carbohydrate intake on heavy training days
- [ ] Don't chronically undereat—inadequate calorie intake impairs recovery and adaptation
- [ ] Immune support: ensure adequate vitamin C and E intake (training can suppress immune function)

## RECOVERY DAY PROTOCOLS

### Active Recovery Options
- [ ] Light walking (20-30 minutes)
- [ ] Easy swimming
- [ ] Gentle cycling
- [ ] Yoga or dedicated mobility session
- [ ] Light rowing at very low intensity (rate 18, minimal pressure)

Target: heart rate 30-60% of maximum; you should be able to hold a conversation comfortably.

### Additional Recovery Tools
- [ ] **Sleep:** minimum 7-9 hours; this is when primary muscle repair occurs
- [ ] **Foam rolling:** can be done daily on tight areas
- [ ] **Contrast showers:** alternating hot/cold (optional, some evidence for circulation benefits)
- [ ] **Massage:** weekly if accessible
- [ ] **Compression garments:** some evidence for reduced soreness (optional)

## QUICK REFERENCE: MINIMUM VIABLE PROTOCOL

**If short on time, prioritise these:**

**Pre-Training (5 minutes minimum):**
1. 2 minutes light cardio
2. Hip flexor stretch (30s each side)
3. World's Greatest Stretch (5 each side)
4. Bodyweight squats (10 reps)
5. Arm circles and shoulder rolls
6. Pick drill progression on erg (3 minutes)

**Post-Training (5 minutes minimum):**
1. 3 minutes easy rowing cool-down
2. Hamstring stretch (30s each side)
3. Hip flexor stretch (30s each side)
4. Child's pose (30s)
5. Drink 500ml fluid immediately
6. Eat carb+protein snack within 30 minutes
